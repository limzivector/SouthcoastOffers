Selling

How to sell your house in 2026

Selling a home doesn’t have to mean months of showings and uncertainty. Here’s the short version of how a cash sale works:

  • Get a preliminary estimate. Enter your address and a few details to see a cash range in minutes.
  • Schedule a quick assessment. We do a short photo or video walkthrough — no cleaning or repairs required.
  • Review your final offer. You’ll get a clear, no-obligation offer with every fee spelled out.
  • Pick your closing date. Choose a day that works for you, typically two weeks to two months out.

The biggest advantage is certainty: a confirmed buyer and a confirmed date, with no financing fall-throughs. Start with a free estimate →

Selling

Cash offer vs. listing with an agent

Listing on the open market can fetch the highest possible price, but it comes with prep, showings, agent commissions, and weeks (or months) of uncertainty. A cash sale trades a bit of that top-end price for speed and certainty.

A cash offer usually makes sense when you value a guaranteed close, want to skip repairs and showings, or need to move on a specific timeline. Listing usually makes sense when your home is in strong condition and you have time to wait for the right buyer.

The right answer depends on your situation — try our net proceeds calculator to compare what you’d actually walk away with under each path.

Selling

Understanding your offer & fees

A cash offer is built from your home’s estimated market value, minus the costs of buying, maintaining, and reselling it. Here’s what typically shows up in an offer breakdown:

  • Service charge: a transparent percentage (commonly around 5%) that covers our costs and risk.
  • Estimated closing costs: standard title and transfer fees, often around 1%.
  • Repair costs: instead of asking you to fix anything, we estimate it and account for it up front.

What you have left after those items — and after paying off any remaining mortgage — is your net proceeds. We show every line before you commit.

Pricing

How home value estimates work

An online estimate blends your home’s size, type, and condition with local price trends to produce a likely value range. It’s a useful starting point — but it’s only as good as the data it has.

A real offer accounts for much more: recent comparable sales on your street, your home’s specific upgrades and layout, and a quick assessment of its condition. That’s why an estimate is a range, not a promise. Try the home value estimator →

Buying with a self-guided tour

Self-guided touring lets you visit a home on your own schedule, without coordinating around an agent’s calendar. You pick a day and time, verify who you are, and get check-in details to let yourself in.

It’s a low-pressure way to really take your time in a space. When you find the one, you can save it, run the numbers with a mortgage estimate, and move forward when you’re ready. Browse homes to tour →

Buying

How much house can you afford?

A common starting rule is to keep your total monthly housing payment — principal, interest, taxes, and insurance — within a comfortable share of your gross monthly income. Your down payment and interest rate move that number a lot.

The best way to get a feel for it is to play with the inputs: change the price, down payment, and rate and watch the monthly payment update. Our mortgage calculator does exactly that, including estimated taxes and insurance.
